Mind the Map:

Charting Africa’s Critical Mineral Partnerships

This interactive map — and its accompanying report — depict bi- and multilateral agreements made with African countries to access their green or “critical” minerals. These minerals, essential for the energy transition, have emerged as a key focus of geopolitical strategy in recent years.

This strategic importance of critical minerals is rising, in part, because of the growing frequency of supply chain disruptions, coupled with intensifying geopolitical competition over future markets for green energy products. As a consequence, multilateral and state-to-state agreements regarding the production, access and processing of critical minerals are on the rise. Africa’s mineral wealth has attracted significant interest, making African states key players in these partnerships. These agreements – often called “mineral partnerships,” “MoUs,” or “cooperation agreements” – vary considerably in their content, format, and key actors. They may include provisions for value chain integration, joint ventures, and knowledge sharing amongst other areas.

Information about these partnerships is not easily accessible, and their broader context is often unclear. APRI conducted an extensive online search using multiple search engines and databases to surface this information. The search was scoped to include G20 and BRICS+ member states, as well as states with a strong mining sector, such as Chile, Cuba and Venezuela. Although the search has yielded a significant number of partnerships, it is not exhaustive. Information will be regularly updated as our understanding of these partnerships broadens.

The map is best explored in conjunction with an accompanying report (accessible via the link below), which contains a discussion of the most salient bilateral agreements with African states.
